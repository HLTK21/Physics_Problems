# 📘 Energy & Momentum Problem – Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Given:
- Mass 1: \( m_1 = 0.5 \, \text{kg} \)
- Mass 2: \( m_2 = 1.5 \, \text{kg} \)
- Height: \( h = 3.0 \, \text{m} \)
- Gravity: \( g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2 \)
- The track is frictionless
- The second block is initially at rest
- The collision is perfectly inelastic (they stick together)

---

# 🧠 Key Definitions & Formulas

## 1. Conservation of Mechanical Energy

When there is no friction:

$$

mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2

$$

This means potential energy at the top converts fully into kinetic energy at the bottom.

---

## 2. Velocity from Energy

From the equation above:

$$

v = \sqrt{2gh}

$$

---

## 3. Conservation of Momentum

In a collision with no external forces:

$$

m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f

$$

---

# 🚀 Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔸 Step 1: Find velocity at the bottom

The block starts from height \( h = 3.0 \, \text{m} \).  
Using energy conservation:

$$

v = \sqrt{2gh}

$$

Substitute values:

$$

v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3}

$$

$$

v = \sqrt{58.8} \approx 7.67 \, \text{m/s}

$$

👉 This is the velocity just before the collision.

---

## 🔸 Step 2: Apply conservation of momentum

Before collision:
- First block moves with \( v_1 = 7.67 \, \text{m/s} \)
- Second block is at rest → \( v_2 = 0 \)

$$

m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f

$$

Substitute values:

$$

0.5 \cdot 7.67 + 1.5 \cdot 0 = (0.5 + 1.5)v_f

$$

$$

3.835 = 2v_f

$$

---

## 🔸 Step 3: Solve for final velocity

$$

v_f = \frac{3.835}{2}

$$

$$

v_f \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}

$$

---

# ✅ Final Answer

$$

\boxed{v_f \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}}

$$

---

# 💡 Extra Insight

- The collision is **perfectly inelastic**, so:
  - Momentum is conserved ✅
  - Kinetic energy is NOT conserved ❌
- Some energy is lost (converted into heat, deformation, etc.)
