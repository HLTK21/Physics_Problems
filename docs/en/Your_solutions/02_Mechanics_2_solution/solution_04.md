# Physics Problem – Step by Step Solution
 
## 🧠 Key Definitions & Formulas
 
### 1. Mechanical Energy Conservation

When no non-conservative forces (like friction) act:
 
$$
 
E_{initial} = E_{final}
 
$$

For gravitational potential energy converting into kinetic energy:
 
$$
 
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
 
$$
 
---
 
### 2. Momentum Conservation (Perfectly Inelastic Collision)
 
When two objects stick together after collision:
 
$$
 
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
 
$$
 
---
 
## 📊 Given Data
 
- Mass of first block:  

  $$ m_1 = 0.5 \, \text{kg} $$
 
- Mass of second block:  

  $$ m_2 = 1.5 \, \text{kg} $$
 
- Height:  

  $$ h = 3.0 \, \text{m} $$
 
- Initial velocity of second block:  

  $$ v_2 = 0 $$
 
- Gravity:  

  $$ g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2 $$
 
---
 
## 🚀 Step 1: Find velocity of block before collision
 
Using energy conservation:
 
$$
 
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
 
$$
 
Cancel mass \( m \):
 
$$
 
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
 
$$
 
Solve for \( v \):
 
$$
 
v = \sqrt{2gh}
 
$$
 
Substitute values:
 
$$
 
v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}
 
$$
 
$$
 
v = \sqrt{58.8}
 
$$
 
$$
 
v \approx 7.67 \, \text{m/s}
 
$$
 
---
 
## 💥 Step 2: Apply Momentum Conservation
 
Before collision:
 
$$
 
p_{initial} = m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2
 
$$
 
Since \( v_2 = 0 \):
 
$$
 
p_{initial} = 0.5 \cdot 7.67
 
$$
 
$$
 
p_{initial} = 3.835 \, \text{kg·m/s}
 
$$
 
---
 
After collision:
 
$$
 
p_{final} = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
 
$$
 
$$
 
p_{final} = (0.5 + 1.5) v_f = 2.0 v_f
 
$$
 
---
 
## ⚖️ Step 3: Solve for final velocity
 
Using conservation:
 
$$
 
3.835 = 2.0 v_f
 
$$
 
$$
 
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
 
$$
 
$$
 
v_f \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}
 
$$
 
---
 
## ✅ Final Answer
 
$$
 
v_f \approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}
 
$$
 
---
 
## 📌 Summary
 
- First block converts potential energy into kinetic energy

- Then undergoes a **perfectly inelastic collision**

- Momentum (not energy) is conserved in collision

- Final velocity of combined mass:
 
👉 **1.92 m/s**
