# Physics Problem: Inelastic Collision After Sliding

## Problem
A 0.5 kg block slides down a frictionless track from a height of 3.0 m.  
At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a 1.5 kg block, which is initially at rest.  

**Find:** The speed of the combined mass just after the collision.

---

## Step 1: Find Speed Before Collision

Using conservation of mechanical energy:

mgh = (1/2)mv²

Mass cancels out:

gh = (1/2)v²

Solve for velocity:

v = √(2gh)

Substitute values:

v = √(2 × 9.8 × 3.0)  
v = √58.8  
v ≈ 7.67 m/s

---

## Step 2: Apply Conservation of Momentum

Since the blocks stick together, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**.

m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = (m₁ + m₂)v_f

Given:
- m₁ = 0.5 kg, v₁ = 7.67 m/s
- m₂ = 1.5 kg, v₂ = 0

Substitute:

(0.5 × 7.67) + (1.5 × 0) = (2.0)v_f

3.835 = 2.0v_f

v_f = 1.92 m/s

---

## Final Answer

**v ≈ 1.9 m/s**

---

## Key Concepts

- Conservation of Mechanical Energy (before collision)
- Conservation of Momentum (during collision)
- Kinetic energy is NOT conserved in inelastic collisions
